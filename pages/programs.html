Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a laptop or computer at home?
+

No. All training is done on academy computers. You only need basic reading and basic school-level English familiarity.

Will I get a job after this course?
+

The program prepares you for entry-level office and data-entry roles. Thalir supports placement guidance and connects strong performers with local employers and panchayat offices.

How is this different from a free computer class?
+

This is a structured, six-month program with clear learning stages, assessments, and NSDC-style certification focused on work-ready skills, not just basics.

Are there competitions or fun activities?
+

Yes! We conduct PowerPoint presentation contests where students design and present their own topics, building confidence and creativity.

Tailoring & Garment Skills

A six-month program that builds strong hand-stitching and machine-based tailoring skills. Learners transition from basic stitching to creating full garments that can be sold or used for home-based businesses.

Who It's For

→Women and youth who want to earn from sewing at home
→School dropouts and homemakers seeking flexible work
→Anyone with interest in stitching but no formal training

Course Syllabus

Tailoring Basics

Hand stitching, taking measurements, cutting and stitching simple items

Machine-based Tailoring

Sewing machine basics, stitching churidars, blouses, salwar kameez

Garment Construction

Patterns, professional fitting, basic alterations and repairs

Value-Added Skills

Simple Aari embroidery, finishing and packaging for sale

Practical Application

Stitching for local customers, Vastrika exhibition participation

💡 Vastrika (Malar Society): Market linkages enabling learners to sell garments in exhibitions and access bulk orders.

What Happens Next

What Happens After the Course?

1. Placement & Job Guidance

Identify interests, map local jobs, interview training, employer connections, 3-month follow-up support.

2. Self-Employment Support

Home-based tailoring units, beauty services, freelance digital work, and small business support.

3. Vastrika Market Linkages

Sell stitched garments in exhibitions, access bulk orders, connect with local businesses. Training → Income → Sustainability.

4. Subsidized Tool Support

Sewing machines at ₹10,000–₹13,000 with ₹1,000/month EMI. Repayment starts only after income begins.

5. Leadership & Mentorship

Top performers become peer mentors, support new batches, build leadership and teaching skills.

Nurturing skills and igniting futures for rural youth and women in Tamil Nadu since 2024.
